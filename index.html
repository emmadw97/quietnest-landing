Why Noise Matters During Pregnancy

  • Studies show that excessive noise can increase stress hormones in pregnant women
  • Loud environments may affect fetal heart rate and movement patterns
  • Regular exposure to high noise levels could impact baby's hearing development

How It All Began

It started when my friend Lieselotte was three months pregnant and eager to attend the Core Festival. Her maternal instinct made her research the effects of loud noises on pregnancy, revealing potential risks to fetal development.

This sparked an idea: create a solution that empowers expectant mothers to confidently navigate their daily lives while protecting their unborn children.

Today, QuietNest helps thousands of mothers-to-be make informed decisions about their sound environment, backed by real-time data and scientific research.
